# Rubrics and interpretation guide

Use these defaults when converting assessment evidence into baseline bands.

## Developmental bands

- **Emerging**: the learner needs heavy prompting, shows inconsistent understanding, or can only complete part of the task.
- **Developing**: the learner can complete simpler versions with support, but accuracy, fluency, or independence is not stable yet.
- **Secure**: the learner can complete age-appropriate tasks independently most of the time and explain thinking in simple terms.
- **Advanced**: the learner exceeds the expected task level, transfers skills, and shows strong independence.

## Confidence levels

- **Low**: limited evidence, fatigue, distraction, or only one short task sampled.
- **Medium**: enough evidence across more than one task, but performance was somewhat variable.
- **High**: repeated, consistent performance across multiple tasks or over time.

## Core language subskills

### Oral language and listening
Look for following instructions, literal and inferential answers, and retelling in sequence.

Support-need indicators:
- misses key details in spoken directions
- struggles to retell events in order
- limited expressive vocabulary relative to task demands

### Reading
Look for decoding, fluency, comprehension, and summarizing.

Support-need indicators:
- accurate with isolated words but weak connected-text meaning
- guesses from context without attending to the full word
- understands orally but not from independent reading

### Writing
Look for sentence completeness, punctuation, idea organization, and stamina.

Support-need indicators:
- has ideas orally but cannot sustain them in writing
- loses track of sentence structure while composing
- avoids writing through minimal responses or fatigue

## Maths subskills

### Number sense and operations
Look for efficient counting strategies, fact recall, place value understanding, and checking reasonableness.

Support-need indicators:
- counts everything from one for simple sums
- inconsistent place value in two- and three-digit numbers
- weak estimation and no sense-checking of answers

### Word problems
Look for extracting relevant information, choosing an operation, representing thinking, and explaining the answer.

Support-need indicators:
- can compute but cannot map the story to the maths
- language load causes breakdown even when arithmetic is possible

## Science subskills

Look for noticing patterns, making predictions, explaining change, using vocabulary, and describing a simple fair test.

Support-need indicators:
- observations remain vague and not evidence-based
- explanations rely on memorized phrases without understanding
- difficulty connecting a practical activity to a verbal explanation

## Profile cautions

Use the profile to inform scaffolds, not labels.
